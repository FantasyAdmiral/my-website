The doomsday algorithm was developed by mathematician John Conway in the 1970s. It is a neat trick that can allow one to determine the day of the week of any given date. It can be done quickly to impress others when mastered.
Beware this is math heavy.

Here are the main steps:

Step 1

Grasp the doomsday pattern

Normal years

  • 1/3
  • 2/28
  • 4/4
  • 6/6
  • 8/8
  • 10/10
  • 12/12
  • 5/9
  • 9/5
  • 7/11
  • 11/7

Leap years

  • 1/4
  • 2/29

Other dates

  • 1/31 or 1/32
  • 2/14 (Valentine's Day) or 2/15
  • 3/14 (Pi Day)
  • 7/4 (Independence Day)
  • 10/31 (Halloween)
  • 12/26 (Boxing Day)

When calculating, travel between these days and the given date.

Step 2

Translate days of the week to numbers

Day of the Week Sunday Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday
Number 0 1 2 3 4 5 6
Hint "Noneday" "Oneday" "Twosday" "Threes-a-day" "Foursday" "Fiveday" "Six-a-day"

Step 3

Memorize anchor doomsdays

  1. 1700 – 0 (Sunday)
  2. 1800 – 5 (Friday)
  3. 2000 – 2 (Tuesday)
  4. 1900 – 3 (Wednesday)
Pattern repeats

Step 4

Learn how the doomsday changes from year to year

Each year, the doomsday moves forward one day. Each leap year, the doomsday moves forward two days.
Importantly, the doomsday moves forward one day every 12 years.

Step 5

Put it all together!
